abstract: |
  Modern ML-based provenance intrusion detection systems (PIDSes) struggle with
  concept drift as advanced persistent threats (APTs) evolve over time. We
  introduce TIDAL, a PIDS that addresses this challenge with a multi-head
  Transformer architecture and a pre-train/fine-tune workflow that learns
  evolving attack patterns from limited data while preventing catastrophic
  forgetting. TIDAL outperforms state-of-the-art systems under concept drift,
  achieving substantially higher recall and precision on new attacks with less
  training data, while retaining strong recall on previous attacks.
